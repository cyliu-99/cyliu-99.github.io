\[ \mathrm{d}U=\mathrm{d}Q-p\mathrm{d}V \] \[ k_BT^2C_V=\langle E^2\rangle-\langle E\rangle^2 \]

L.H.S. Response function, represents compressibility
R.H.S. fluctuation of energy, represents number of particles

\[ \frac{\sqrt{\langle (\Delta E)^2\rangle}}{\langle E\rangle}= \sqrt{k_BT^2 C_V}\sim \frac{1}{\sqrt{N}}\quad N\to \infty \]

In the limit of large \(N\), canonical ensemble \(\to\) micro canonical ensemble

temperature \(\sim\) kinetic energy
If the particle number is small, for example, \(N=2\), the curve of kinetic varied greatly.

If the particle number is large, the kinetic energy would be constant with minor fluctuation.

Example: Ideal gas \(N,V,T\) \[ H=\sum\limits_{i=1}^N\frac{p_i^2}{2m} \]

\[ \begin{aligned} Q_N(T,V)&=\frac{1}{N!}\frac{1}{h^{3N}} \int e^{-\frac{1}{2m}\sum\limits_i p_i^2 }\prod\limits_{i=1}^N \mathrm{d}^3q_i\mathrm{d}^3p_i\\ &=\frac{1}{N!}\left[\frac{V}{h^3}(2\pi mk_B T)^{1/2}\right]^N\\ &=\frac{1}{N!}\left[Q_1(V,T)\right]^N \end{aligned} \]

\[ Q_1=\frac{V}{\lambda_T^2},\lambda_T=\frac{h}{\sqrt{2\pi mk_BT}} \]

\(Q_1(V,T)\) is the partition function for a single particle.

\[ a\gg \frac{h}{\sqrt{2\pi m k_BT}}, \quad h\to 0 \]

quantum effects could be neglected

\(Q_N=\frac{1}{N!}Q_1^N\), shows statistical independence of gas particles. And \(\frac{1}{N!}\) shows extensivity.

How does \(N\) ensure extensivity \[ N!\approx \left(\frac{N}{e}\right)^N \]

extensive quantities for short ranged interacting system. \[ E_{\text{tot}}=\sum\limits_i E_i,\quad \text{No marginal effects} \]

If it's long ranged interaction, total energy should incorporate interaction energy as well

The effects of the shape of the margin on the system.

If the shape of the box is circle or square, the trace of a particle moving inside would be fixed, unable to traverse the box, therefore unable to reach equilibrium. If there exists a shape that could make the particle traverse the box.

\[ p=-\left(\frac{\partial A}{\partial V}\right)_{N,T}=\frac{Nk_B T}{V} \]

\[ g(E)=\left\{\begin{aligned} \frac{V^N}{N!}\left(\frac{2\pi m}{h^2}\right)^{\frac{3N}{2}} \frac{E^{\frac{3}{2}N-1}}{\frac{3}{2}N-1} \quad & E\ge 0\\ 0 \quad & E<0 \end{aligned}\right. \]

\[ \rho(E)\mathrm{d}E \sim e^{-\beta E} g(E)\mathrm{d}E \]

\[ \frac{\partial e^{-\beta E}g(E)}{\partial E}=0 \]

\[ \begin{aligned} E&= \left(\frac{3}{2}N-1\right)k_BT\\ &=\frac{3}{2}k_BTN=U \end{aligned} \]

Hamiltonian of one particle \[ H=\frac{p_x^2}{2m}+\frac{p_y^2}{2m}+\frac{p_z^2}{2m} \]

\[ \langle H\rangle = \frac{1}{2}k_BT\times 3 \]

Example 2 ideal gas
\(N\) "red" atoms,
\(N\) "blue" atoms,
\(N\) "green" atoms

atoms of the same colour are indistinguishable, atoms of different color are distinguishable,
a) entropy under canonical ensemble
b) what is the atoms are of the same color

Solution:
mircrostate \(\rho(q,p,\text{color})\)

\(N\) atoms of the same color. \[ Q_N = \frac{1}{N!} \left(\frac{V}{\lambda_T}\right)^N\to A_N=Nf(T)-Nk_BT\ln \left(\frac{eV}{N}\right) \]

\[ f(T)= \left(\frac{mk_BT}{2\pi \hbar^2}\right)^{\frac{3}{2}} \]

\(Q=Q_N^3\)

\[ \begin{aligned} A &= 3Nf(T) - 3Nk_BT\ln \left(\frac{eV}{N}\right)\\ S &= 3Nk_B\ln \left(\frac{eV}{3N}\right)-3Nf'(T) \end{aligned} \]

  1. \[ S_1 = 3Nk_B\ln \left(\frac{eV}{3N}\right)-3Nf'(T) \]

\[ S-S_1=3N\ln 3 = \ln 3^{3N} = \ln \Omega_c \]

\(3\) is from 3 kinds of colors

\[ S=S_1+S_c \]

\(S\): microstate \((q,p,\text{color})\) \(S_1\): \((q,p)\) \(S_c\): \((color)\)

total entropy \(S=S(d.o.f 1)+S(d.o.f.2)\)

\[ C_V \sim T\frac{\partial S}{\partial T} \]
